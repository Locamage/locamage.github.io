# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

General visitors who arrive with little context and need to understand what Locamage does quickly: people curious about satellite imagery, AI, geospatial intelligence, disaster response, agriculture, climate, insurance, or research workflows. Some will be technical, but the default reader should not need GIS expertise or procurement context to follow the page.

## Product Purpose

Locamage makes satellite imagery easier to question and understand. The public site should explain the core promise in plain language: bring an image, ask what matters, and get useful answers without specialized GIS tooling. Success means a first-time visitor can grasp the product, trust that the capability is serious, and know how to try the demo or request access.

## Brand Personality

Precise, credible, and operational. The voice should feel expert without becoming opaque: clear claims, concrete examples, and visual restraint that supports trust. The brand can feel advanced and technically capable, but it should remain legible to non-specialists.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS polish: gradient blobs, rounded card walls, cheerful AI mascots, chat-bubble tropes, and vague "unlock insights" copy. Avoid consumer AI gloss that makes the product feel like a novelty. Also avoid an overly dark, classified-terminal mood that shuts out general visitors; the requested light-mode direction should feel open, serious, and readable rather than sterile or washed out.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with plain evidence: show concrete imagery, examples, and outcomes before abstract claims.
2. Make advanced capability feel accessible: explain geospatial intelligence in language a curious first-time visitor can understand.
3. Keep credibility visible: every visual flourish should support trust, precision, or comprehension.
4. Treat light mode as intentional: the interface should feel clear and operational in daylight, not like a dark system merely inverted.
5. Prioritize clear next steps: demo, access, and contact paths should work for visitors who are ready now and visitors who need more context first.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG AA as the baseline, including 4.5:1 contrast for body and placeholder text, visible focus states, keyboard-accessible navigation, descriptive image alt text, and reduced-motion alternatives for animation. Avoid relying on color alone to communicate state or meaning.
